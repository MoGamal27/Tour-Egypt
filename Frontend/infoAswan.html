The Jewel of the Nile

Abu Simbel Temple

Carved into a mountainside, the twin temples of Abu Simbel, built by Ramesses II, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for their colossal statues and the biannual Sun Festival when sunlight illuminates the inner sanctuary.

  • Location: Near Lake Nasser, 280 km south of Aswan, Egypt
  • Built: 13th century BCE (c. 1264–1244 BCE, reign of Ramesses II)
  • Features: Great Temple of Ramesses II, Small Temple of Nefertari, Sun Festival (Feb 22 & Oct 22), Sound and Light Show
  • Visiting Hours: 5:00 AM – 6:00 PM (daily, last entry 5:30 PM)
  • Ticket Price: 600 EGP for foreigners, 300 EGP for Egyptians
Location

Philae Temple

Dedicated to the goddess Isis, the Philae Temple, relocated to Agilkia Island after the Aswan Dam’s construction, is a masterpiece of Ptolemaic architecture, accessible by boat and known for its serene island setting.

  • Location: Agilkia Island, Aswan, Egypt
  • Built: Ptolemaic Period (c. 280–68 BCE)
  • Features: Temple of Isis, Kiosk of Trajan, Sound and Light Show, intricate reliefs
  • Visiting Hours: 7:00 AM – 4:00 PM (daily, last entry 3:30 PM)
  • Ticket Price: 450 EGP for foreigners, 220 EGP for Egyptians
Location

Nubian Village

Colorful villages along the Nile, such as Gharb Soheil, offer an immersive experience of Nubian culture, with vibrant houses, local crafts, and warm hospitality, accessible by felucca or motorboat.

  • Location: Gharb Soheil, West Bank, Aswan, Egypt
  • Built: Traditional settlements, modern tourism since 20th century
  • Features: Painted houses, Nubian crafts, cultural performances, camel rides
  • Visiting Hours: Open access (guided tours typically 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM)
  • Ticket Price: Varies by tour (approx. 200–500 EGP for boat and guide)
Location

Aswan High Dam

A monumental feat of modern engineering, the Aswan High Dam controls the Nile’s floods, creates Lake Nasser, and generates hydroelectric power, shaping Egypt’s agricultural and economic landscape.

  • Location: South of Aswan, Egypt
  • Built: 1960–1970 (inaugurated 1971)
  • Features: 111-meter-high dam, Lake Nasser, visitor center, panoramic views
  • Visiting Hours: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM (daily, last entry 4:30 PM)
  • Ticket Price: 200 EGP for foreigners, 100 EGP for Egyptians
Location

Aswan Botanical Garden

Located on Kitchener’s Island, the Aswan Botanical Garden is a tranquil oasis featuring exotic plants, palm trees, and colorful flowers, ideal for leisurely walks and felucca visits.

  • Location: Kitchener’s Island, Aswan, Egypt
  • Built: Established 1899 by Lord Kitchener
  • Features: Rare tropical plants, walking paths, Nile views, birdwatching
  • Visiting Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (daily, last entry 4:30 PM)
  • Ticket Price: 50 EGP for foreigners, 25 EGP for Egyptians (felucca ride extra)
