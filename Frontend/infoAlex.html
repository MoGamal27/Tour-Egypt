Qaitbay Citadel

A 15th-century fortress built to defend Alexandria against Ottoman invasions, the Qaitbay Citadel is a stunning example of Mamluk military architecture, perched on the Mediterranean coast at the site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

  • Location: Eastern Harbor, Alexandria, Egypt
  • Built:1477–1479 CE (Mamluk Sultanate, under Sultan Al-Ashraf Qaitbay)
  • Main Structures:Main fortress, defensive walls, inner chambers, mosque
  • 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (daily, may vary seasonally
  • Ticket Price:60 EGP for foreigners
Location

Bibliotheca Alexandrina

A modern revival of the ancient Library of Alexandria, the Bibliotheca Alexandrina is a beacon of knowledge, housing millions of books, a planetarium, museums, and research centers, celebrating Egypt's cultural and intellectual heritage.

  • Location: El-Shatby, Alexandria, Egypt
  • Built: 2002 (inaugurated October 16, 2002)
  • Features: Main library, planetarium, conference center, four museums (Antiquities, Manuscripts, Sadat, History of Science)
  • Visiting Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM (Sunday to Thursday), 2:00 PM – 7:00 PM (Friday), 12:00 PM – 4:00 PM (Saturday)
  • Ticket Price: 70 EGP for foreigners, 10 EGP for Egyptians (additional fees for museums and planetarium)
Location

Roman Amphitheatre

The Roman Amphitheatre in Alexandria, located at Kom El Dekka, is the only known Roman theatre in Egypt. Discovered by chance in 1960, this 4th-century AD structure showcases Greco-Roman architectural brilliance, once hosting musical performances, poetry competitions, and public assemblies.

  • Location: Kom El Dekka, Alexandria, Egypt
  • Built: 4th century AD (used until 7th century AD)
  • Features: Marble seating for 600–800 spectators, orchestra section, Roman baths, Villa of the Birds with mosaics
  • Visiting Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (last entry 4:00 PM)
  • Ticket Price: 80 EGP for foreigners, 40 EGP for Egyptians (free for children under 6, Egyptians over 60, and Egyptians with special needs)
Location

Montazah Palace Gardens

A lush oasis along Alexandria’s Mediterranean coast, the Montazah Palace Gardens span approximately 370 acres, offering serene landscapes, rare trees, and stunning sea views. Built as a royal summer retreat, these gardens surround the historic Montazah Palace, blending natural beauty with architectural elegance.

  • Location: Al Mandarah Bahri, Montazah District, Alexandria, Egypt
  • Built: 1892 (Salamlek Palace by Khedive Abbas II), 1932 (Haramlek Palace by King Fuad I)
  • Features: Landscaped gardens, palm-lined avenues, five beaches (Aida, Cleopatra, Vanessa, Semiramis, private Helnan Palestine Hotel beach), wading pools, fountains, Italian-Gothic bridge
  • Visiting Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (daily, may extend to midnight seasonally)
  • Ticket Price: 25 EGP per person (additional 25 EGP for vehicle parking, 15 EGP for beach access)
Location

Alexandria National Museum

Housed in a restored Italian-style palace, the Alexandria National Museum narrates the rich history of Alexandria and Egypt through over 1,800 artifacts, spanning Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, Coptic, Islamic, and modern eras, offering a vibrant journey through the city’s cultural heritage.

  • Location: 110 El Horreya Rd, Ash Shatibi, Alexandria, Egypt
  • Built: 1926 (palace), inaugurated as museum in 2003
  • Features: Three floors of exhibits (Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, Coptic/Islamic/modern), underwater artifacts, open-air theatre, audio-visual workshop
  • Visiting Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily (10:00 AM on Fridays)
  • Ticket Price: 180 EGP for foreigners, 20 EGP for Egyptians (credit card only, additional fee for photography permit)
