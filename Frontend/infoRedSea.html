Red Sea Governorate

The Tropical Paradise of Egypt

Giftun Island

A premier marine reserve near Hurghada, Giftun Island is renowned for its turquoise waters, white sand beaches like Mahmya and Orange Bay, and vibrant coral reefs, making it a top destination for snorkeling and diving.

  • Location: 11 km east of Hurghada, Red Sea Governorate, Egypt
  • Established: Designated as a marine reserve in 1995
  • Features: Mahmya and Orange Bay beaches, 14 dive sites, snorkeling, eco-tourism, marine life (turtles, dolphins, seagulls)
  • Visiting Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (daily, via boat tours from Hurghada)
  • Ticket Price: Approx. 500–1000 EGP for boat tours (varies by operator)
Location

El Gouna

A luxurious resort town on the Red Sea, El Gouna offers pristine beaches, lagoons, and watersports like kitesurfing and diving, with a vibrant community and eco-friendly architecture inspired by Nubian villages.

  • Location: 20 km north of Hurghada, Red Sea Governorate, Egypt
  • Established: Developed in 1990 by Orascom Development
  • Features: Zeytuna Beach, marinas, 18-hole golf course, diving sites, kitesurfing, restaurants
  • Visiting Hours: Open access (beaches and activities available daily)
  • Ticket Price: Free entry; activities and tours vary (e.g., 500–1500 EGP for diving)
Location

Soma Bay

A coastal resort peninsula south of Hurghada, Soma Bay is a haven for water sports enthusiasts, offering kitesurfing, windsurfing, and diving, alongside luxury resorts and a Gary Player-designed golf course.

  • Location: 45 km south of Hurghada, Red Sea Governorate, Egypt
  • Established: Developed in the 1990s
  • Features: 18-hole golf course, diving sites, kitesurfing, luxury resorts (Sheraton, Kempinski), spa centers
  • Visiting Hours: Open access (beaches and activities available daily)
  • Ticket Price: Free entry; activities vary (e.g., 600–1200 EGP for diving/snorkeling tours)
Location

Hamata Islands

Also known as the Qulaan Archipelago, these remote islands in Wadi El Gemal National Park are a pristine paradise with vibrant coral reefs, mangroves, and opportunities for snorkeling and birdwatching.

  • Location: 130 km south of Marsa Alam, Red Sea Governorate, Egypt
  • Established: Part of Wadi El Gemal National Park, protected since 2003
  • Features: Siyal, Shawarit, Umm al-Shaykh, Mahabis islands, coral reefs, mangroves, birdwatching
  • Visiting Hours: 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM (daily, via boat tours from Hamata marina)
  • Ticket Price: Approx. 700–1500 EGP for boat tours (varies by operator)
Location

Wadi El Gemal

A national park encompassing desert and sea, Wadi El Gemal is known for its untouched marine life, coral reefs, and diverse ecosystems, offering diving, snorkeling, and desert safaris.

  • Location: 40 km south of Marsa Alam, Red Sea Governorate, Egypt
  • Established: Designated as a national park in 2003
  • Features: 450 coral species, 1200 fish species, Sharm el Luli beach, desert safaris, Hamata Islands
  • Visiting Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (daily, via guided tours)
  • Ticket Price: Approx. 200–300 EGP for park entry; tours vary (500–2000 EGP)
